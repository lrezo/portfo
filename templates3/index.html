LIFE

Steven Paul Jobs was born February 24, 1955 to Joanne Carole Schieble and Abdulfattah Jandali in San Francisco, but was adopted by Justin and Clara Jobs from Mountain View, California. His biological parents later married and had a daughter, writer Mona Simpson.

Jobs is most famous as a co-founder, Chairman, and CEO of Apple Inc and former CEO of Pixar Animation Studios. He also founded NeXt after he resigned from Apple in 1985. NeXt was bought by Apple in 1997. Since Disney bought Pixar in 2006, Jobs is also Disney's largest individual shareholder.

Through Apple, the company he co-founded in 1976, Steve Jobs permanently redefined the method, look and feel of personal computing. He was among the first to recognize the vast commercial potential of the graphical user interface, the mouse-driven point-and-click system that pilots virtually every desktop computer today. During the course of a career that spanned almost four decades, he introduced several paradigm-shifting devices, reshaping entire industries in the process.

On October 5, 2011, Jobs passed away after suffering from cancer, Jobs was 56.



INVENTIONS

Macintosh

In 1985 the Macintosh was the first computer that has an OS the “MacOS” with graphic interface and standard mouse and keyboard, it was not working with a hard disk but with several floppy disk.

iPhone

In 2007, he reinvented the classical phone with a hardware keyboard and buttons in a interactive smartphone with a touchscreen display without any physical button than the home button this is the iPhone 1 until the iPhone 4.

iPod

In 2001 Apple has invented the iPod a whole new category of digital music player that lets you put your entire music collection in your pocket and listen to it wherever you go.
